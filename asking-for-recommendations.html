Asking for recommendations

How to approach someone for a recommendation

When it comes to asking someone for a recommendation, it's important to approach the situation with respect and professionalism. Whether you're seeking a recommendation for a job, school application, or any other opportunity, there are a few key steps to keep in mind.


First and foremost, consider your relationship with the person you're asking for a recommendation. If possible, choose someone who knows you well and can speak to your strengths and qualifications. It's also important to give them plenty of notice before requesting a recommendation, so they have time to write a thoughtful response.


When reaching out to ask for a recommendation, be sure to clearly explain why you're seeking their help and what specific qualities or experiences you would like them to highlight. Provide any necessary information about the opportunity you're applying for, such as deadlines or specific requirements.


It's also important to express gratitude for their time and assistance. Remember that writing a recommendation takes effort on their part, so be sure to thank them sincerely for their support.


Overall, approaching someone for a recommendation is all about being respectful, clear, and appreciative. By following these guidelines, you can increase your chances of receiving a positive and helpful recommendation that will support your goals.

Tips for providing background information to the recommender

When asking someone to provide a recommendation for you, it's important to give them some background information so they can write a meaningful and accurate letter on your behalf. Here are some tips for providing background information to the recommender:




  1. Start by reminding the recommender of your relationship with them. Whether they are a former boss, colleague, teacher, or mentor, make sure they understand how you know each other and in what capacity.




  2. Provide specific details about the position or program you are applying for. Give the recommender information about the job description, requirements, and any specific skills or experiences that are relevant to the position.




  3. Share your career goals and aspirations with the recommender. Let them know what you hope to achieve in your future career and how this opportunity aligns with your long-term plans.




  4. Give examples of your accomplishments and strengths. Share specific projects you have worked on, challenges you have overcome, and any leadership roles you have taken on that demonstrate your skills and abilities.




  5. Be honest about any weaknesses or areas where you may need improvement. It's important for the recommender to have a well-rounded view of your abilities so they can provide an accurate assessment of your potential.




By providing this background information to the recommender, you can help them write a strong letter of recommendation that highlights your qualifications, experiences, and potential for success in the role or program you are applying for.

    Examples of specific questions to ask when requesting a recommendation

    When seeking recommendations for a specific topic, it's important to ask thoughtful and targeted questions to ensure you receive the most helpful advice. Instead of simply asking for general recommendations, consider asking specific questions that will help guide your search.


    For example, you could ask for recommendations on sources that provide a comprehensive overview of the topic, or inquire about any recent research or developments in the field. You might also want to ask for suggestions on experts or professionals who have specialized knowledge in the area.


    Additionally, consider asking for recommendations on books, articles, websites, or other resources that offer different perspectives on the topic. This can help you gain a more well-rounded understanding and make informed decisions moving forward.


    By asking specific questions when requesting recommendations, you'll be better equipped to find the information and insights you need to delve deeper into your chosen topic. So next time you're looking for advice or guidance, don't hesitate to ask detailed and focused questions that will lead you in the right direction.

    Etiquette and follow-up after receiving a recommendation

    Etiquette plays a key role in how we navigate through various social situations, including asking for recommendations. When someone takes the time to provide us with a recommendation, it's important to show gratitude and follow up appropriately.


    After receiving a recommendation, it's essential to express our thanks to the person who offered it. A simple thank you goes a long way in showing appreciation for their time and effort. Whether it was for a book, restaurant, movie, or anything else, letting them know that their recommendation was valuable to us is crucial.


    Following up on the recommendation is also an important step in showing respect and consideration towards the person who provided it. This could involve trying out the suggestion and sharing our feedback with them, whether positive or negative. It shows that we value their opinion and are willing to engage with their suggestion.


    In some cases, following up could also mean asking for further recommendations based on their initial suggestion. This not only keeps the conversation going but also signals that we trust and value their judgment. It opens up opportunities for meaningful discussions and strengthens our relationship with them.


    Ultimately, etiquette in asking for recommendations involves being polite, appreciative, and responsive. By acknowledging their effort, expressing gratitude, and following up accordingly, we not only show respect towards the person providing the recommendation but also foster positive relationships built on trust and mutual understanding.
